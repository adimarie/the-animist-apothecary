Community Economic Model

Sacred Reciprocity

A return to the way healing has always moved — in circles, not straight lines.

The Practice of Fair Exchange

Healing belongs to the whole community

In traditional ways, healers were supported by the community because the health of one is the health of all. That truth still guides my work.

My offerings require a deep investment of energy, time, and training, and I am committed to promoting economic justice. We do not all walk the same path of resources.

I believe that healing and spiritual counsel should be accessible — while honoring the real costs of sustaining this work.

Returning to What Was

Community-based care is not a new idea

Long before healing became an industry, it lived inside relationships. The healer knew the family. The community fed the healer. The medicine moved in circles — not transactions. No one was turned away, and no one carried the work alone.

This is not a radical concept. It is an ancient one. Across cultures and throughout human history, the people who tended the body, the spirit, and the unseen dimensions of life were held by a web of mutual care. They were not vendors. They were vital organs of the collective body — and the community understood that when the healer was nourished, the whole village was well.

Somewhere along the way, this relationship was severed. Healing became a commodity. Access became contingent on ability to pay a fixed price. And practitioners — especially those carrying Indigenous, earth-based, and body-centered traditions — were left navigating an economy that was never designed to hold the kind of work they do.

The sliding scale is one way of remembering. It is a humble attempt to restore the circle — to create an economic container that honors the real diversity of people's lives while sustaining the practitioner's ability to keep showing up with depth, presence, and devotion.

Three things this model asks of us

I

Honesty

Each person is invited to reflect truthfully on where they sit in the landscape of resources — not where they wish they sat, and not where shame or habit tells them to sit. Just the truth of right now.

II

Trust

I do not ask for proof of income. I do not audit your life. This model runs on the same currency that the healing work itself runs on: trust between two people choosing to be in relationship.

III

Participation

Every tier is a form of participation in the health of the whole. Those who give more make it possible for others to receive. Those who receive carry the medicine forward in their own way. No role is lesser.

This is not charity, and it is not a discount. It is an invitation to participate in an older kind of economy — one where the exchange itself is part of the healing.

A Gentle Word on Value

What it takes to hold this work

I want to speak honestly about something that the wellness world often dances around: what it actually costs — in every sense — to do this kind of work well.

The offerings I bring are the result of over sixteen years of devoted study, initiation, and practice across multiple lineages, disciplines, and continents. They draw on traditions that my ancestors preserved at great personal cost — traditions that were outlawed, driven underground, and carried forward in whispers and in the body's own memory.

This work asks everything of me. It asks my body to hold space for hours. It asks my nervous system to navigate realms that are not easy to navigate. It asks my heart to remain open in the presence of immense suffering. And it asks my life to be organized around the kind of devotion that doesn't clock in and out.

This is skilled, technical, sacred labor. It involves the same rigor as any professional discipline — and an additional dimension that most professional disciplines do not ask: the willingness to be changed by the work, every single time.

Every dollar that comes to me nourishes a living web. It supports the elders I continue to learn from. It funds the materials, the travel, the ceremonies, the ongoing education. It provides shelter, food, and the basic stability that allows me to keep showing up with the depth this work requires.

"Your evolution serves everyone." — Katharine Hargreaves

I share this not to justify my prices, but to gently illuminate what lives behind them. When you engage with this model, you are not just paying for a session. You are participating in the survival and flourishing of a living tradition — and in the kind of economy where care flows in circles rather than straight lines.

Choose Your Tier

Four pathways of reciprocity

I do not require income verification. I trust you to choose honestly. Specific rates for each offering are listed on their respective pages — below is the framework for how the tiers work across all of my services.

Patronage & Stewardship

Patron Rate

This is for those who feel resourced and want to offer abundance, stewardship, and patronage — not just paying for a session, but helping support the work in the world. This tier is a blend of gratitude and generosity, and it is one of the ways the accessibility rates are possible at all.

This tier is for you if you:

  • ◈ Carry wealth, savings, or significant financial ease
  • ◈ Want to invest in the survival of this medicine beyond your own session
  • ◈ Feel called to steward and sponsor the broader healing work
  • ◈ Wish to directly make accessibility sessions possible for others

Your patronage keeps the circle whole. This is how community care sustains itself.

Full Value

Abundance Rate

This reflects the real cost of my time, materials, preparation, and the depth of skill I bring. It is the standard "I can meet the full value of this support" tier, and it helps keep the community model steady and sustainable.

This tier is for you if you:

  • ◈ Can comfortably meet your basic needs
  • ◈ Own property, hold savings, or have expendable income
  • ◈ Can afford wants, travel, and investments
  • ◈ Want to honor the full value of the work you receive

This is where the true value of the work is met — honoring what it takes to offer it at this depth.

Mutual Support

Community Rate

This is for people who are part of the broader web: community members, peers and other holistic health and wellness professionals, folks in closer proximity to the work of healing, or those in a more mutual relationship in alliance with these modalities. This is the rate where I can break even.

This tier is for you if you:

  • ◈ Are a fellow practitioner, healer, or wellness professional
  • ◈ Meet basic needs with some ease but the full rate is a stretch
  • ◈ Are in mutual or ongoing relationship with this work
  • ◈ Have a steady income with some expendable funds

Supportive, fair, and sustainable — where the exchange finds its balance.

Scholarship

Accessibility Rate

This acknowledges financial hardship and systemic barriers. I offer a limited number of sessions at this tier. To be transparent: it does not fully cover the true cost of my time and resources. It is essentially a scholarship rate, and I hold it because I believe care should still be reachable during hard seasons.

This tier is for you if you:

  • ◈ Often worry about meeting basic needs
  • ◈ Have little or no expendable income
  • ◈ Qualify for assistance or live paycheck-to-paycheck
  • ◈ Would face genuine hardship paying at higher tiers

If this rate is still out of reach, please reach out so we can find a sustainable path together.

A Living Model

The circle is always moving

One of the things I love most about this model is that it breathes. It is not a fixed identity — it is a living relationship with your current season.

Sometimes someone comes to this work during a time when resources are thin, and they receive at the accessibility tier with grace. Later, when life opens, they choose to give at abundance or patron — not because they owe anything, but because they want to give back to the web that held them.

This is how community care has always worked. The tides go in and out. People give when they can. People receive when they need to. And the circle holds because everyone participates with honesty and good faith.

Those paying at the patron and abundance tiers directly make it possible for accessibility sessions to exist. This is not abstract — it is the literal mechanism by which the work remains available to people in every season of life. It is community-based economics in its most intimate, human form.

If you would like to go deeper into this model or need help finding a sustainable path, I warmly invite you to reach out so we can talk it through together.

The Ecology of Reciprocity

This work is not a transaction.
It is a sacred exchange.

It is a living exchange within a larger ecosystem. When we care for one part of the ecosystem, the whole flourishes. When you pay me, you are nourishing the web of elders, teachers, spirits, and community to whom I am accountable — and you are participating in a future where medicine keepers do not have to choose between poverty and their calling.

Please reflect honestly:

Does paying the full rate feel like a natural stretch — or genuine hardship?

Could you contribute more to nourish the broader community?

Would paying less allow you to access this work without sacrificing essentials?

Whatever tier you choose, you are welcome here.
Together, we keep the circle strong.
May we build an economy of healing, honesty, and mutual care.
